Listening In Nature


Listening in Nature is one of the pillars of a new Earth conversation. It conveys our sense that practices of slowing down, listening, becoming observant and available, and awakening to the magic of living ecosystems is vitally important as we navigate the realities of a warming world. Events and activities organized under the banner of Listening in Nature are designed to cultivate appreciation and exploration of the natural world and to remind ourselves of the interdependence of all living things.

Each semester A new Earth conversation organizes a day of Listening in Nature with a variety of opportunities for faculty, students, staff, and community members to slow down and experience the natural world with renewed attention. We also offer support to faculty who seek to incorporate immersive experiences in nature into their teaching.



As part of Listening in Nature, we encourage you to spend time in the natural spaces around you. On this page we have compiled some additional resources to guide you. Here you will find information about local green spaces in Worcester, creative prompts for self-guided nature exploration, and resources to expand your listening practice.

As you engage with the natural world that surround us in Main South and throughout your community,  we invite you to share these moments with all of us at Clark using the hashtags #ClarkNEC and #ClarkLIN.
